# inst.beta

## Purpose

This file is the second coded institutional overlay draft built from the Logos upstream architecture.

It is intentionally written as a reusable coded instantiation rather than a public-facing case memo tied to a named institution.

It should be read together with:
- `docs/governance/tradition-composition-profile.md`
- `docs/governance/institution-overlay.md`
- `docs/governance/hitl-decision-rules.md`
- `docs/governance/build-priority-policy.md`
- `docs/airca/README.md` in the companion AIRCA repository

## Overlay identity

### Coded name
`inst.beta`

### Institution type
A public-facing advocacy institution operating in high-conflict, high-visibility environments.

### Institutional pattern
This institution operates through a mixed environment that includes:
- executive leadership
- legal or policy strategy functions
- public-positioning pressures
- donor and supporter visibility
- adversarial external environments
- recurring escalation and review burdens

## Inherited upstream profile

`inst.beta` should inherit from a Christian tradition composition profile that strongly emphasizes:
- truth and moral clarity
- human dignity
- authority and accountability
- public witness
- prudence under conflict
- institutional courage without mission drift
- constraints on technological convenience when judgment stakes are high

At this stage, the exact formal tradition profile may remain provisional.
The important point is that `inst.beta` is downstream from a reusable theological source architecture rather than from a local strategic posture alone.

## Mission shape

The mission pattern of `inst.beta` is:
- public witness under conflict
- advocacy or decision support in contested environments
- recurring need to distinguish prudence from passivity
- recurring need to distinguish courage from rhetorical overreach
- repeated exposure to technology temptations that promise speed while increasing misclassification, overclaiming, or weakened accountability

## Governance environment

The main governance pressures on `inst.beta` include:
- executive pressure for strategic clarity and speed
- high need for review discipline under conflict
- public credibility concerns
- donor and supporter interpretation pressures
- potential legal, reputational, and mission risk from poorly governed AI use
- need to preserve theological and institutional coherence in adversarial settings

## Stakeholder map

The main stakeholder groups are:
- executive leadership
- strategic and legal operators
- communications or public-positioning teams
- internal staff
- board or governing authorities where applicable
- donors and supporters
- allied partners
- external audiences and critics

## Recurring decision classes

The recurring decisions most relevant to `inst.beta` include:

### 1. Public-positioning decisions
Examples:
- whether a proposed public framing is mission-faithful
- whether a message is overextended, under-argued, or mistimed
- whether a response should be escalated, delayed, or refined

### 2. Advocacy and escalation decisions
Examples:
- when to act publicly versus remain preparatory
- which issues warrant stronger escalation
- what kind of review is required before commitment

### 3. AI-assisted research and drafting decisions
Examples:
- what kinds of drafting may be AI-assisted
- what requires deeper human review because precision risk is high
- what kinds of machine-generated output should never be published without authority review

### 4. Mission-integrity decisions
Examples:
- whether speed is displacing prudence
- whether institutional language is remaining faithful to source commitments
- whether a tactical move distorts the institution's witness or role

### 5. Governance and trust decisions
Examples:
- what internal review layers are required for public or contested action
- what must remain attributable to named human judgment
- how to preserve decision accountability in fast-moving environments

## Mission-constrained translation layer

`inst.beta` should translate upstream theology into language such as:
- truthfulness
- accountable public witness
- prudent escalation
- role integrity
- human review under conflict
- disciplined advocacy

This translation should remain mission-faithful rather than becoming merely tactical.

## HITL implications

For `inst.beta`, the following should normally require at least `review_required` or stronger:
- AI-assisted drafts for external use
- issue summaries that materially affect public or strategic action
- internal rankings of options in contested settings
- machine-assisted analysis used to narrow public-positioning choices

The following should normally require `escalation_required` or stronger:
- AI-assisted materials that could shape external posture
- any workflow that compresses review in high-conflict cases
- changes to how institutional voice is translated into public action
- changes to evidence-handling rules in contested environments

The following should normally require `authority_commit_required`:
- publication or action based on AI-assisted strategic recommendation in high-stakes cases
- formal institution-wide policy on AI use in advocacy, strategy, or public witness
- adoption of AI-assisted workflows that materially affect public accountability or external credibility

## Prohibited drifts

`inst.beta` should guard against:
- rhetorical acceleration without adequate review
- AI-assisted confidence that exceeds evidence
- machine-shaped positioning masquerading as accountable judgment
- tactical convenience overtaking truthfulness or prudence
- public witness becoming more reactive, abstract, or decontextualized through automation

## Suggested next derived artifacts

The strongest next downstream artifacts for `inst.beta` would be:
- a public-positioning review matrix
- an evidence and attribution policy for AI-assisted drafting
- a strategic escalation map
- a high-conflict HITL review workflow
- a mission-integrity filter for external messaging

## Summary principle

`inst.beta` is a coded overlay for a high-visibility advocacy institution that must preserve truthfulness, prudence, and visible human accountability under conflict, especially where AI could otherwise compress review and distort public action.
