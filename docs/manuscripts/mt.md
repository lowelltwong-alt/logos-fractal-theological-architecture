# Masoretic Text (MT)

## Why this witness matters

The Masoretic Text is one of the central textual witnesses for the Hebrew Bible and therefore one of the foundational manuscript-level nodes for the repository.

For this project, it matters because many modern Old Testament translations stand in close relation to the Masoretic tradition, and because text-level doctrinal or lexical work often assumes a Masoretic base even when that assumption is left unstated.

## Why it belongs in the manuscript layer

The MT should not be confused with:
- the original autograph
- every earlier Hebrew witness
- the only relevant textual tradition

It is a major textual tradition and witness, not a magical bypass around textual criticism.

## Repository significance

The MT is especially important for:
- Genesis 1 and 3 lexical work
- Hebrew term studies
- comparison with Septuagint and Dead Sea Scrolls witnesses where relevant
- identifying where translations depend on a Masoretic base

## Interpretive caution

The repository should treat the MT as a primary and highly important witness, while still allowing comparison with:
- Septuagint traditions
- Dead Sea Scrolls evidence
- later text-critical notes where variants matter
