# Septuagint (LXX)

## Why this witness matters

The Septuagint is one of the most important ancient versional witnesses for the Old Testament and therefore belongs in the repository's manuscript and textual-tradition layer.

For this project, it matters because it is not merely a historical curiosity. It is deeply relevant to:
- textual comparison
- patristic interpretation
- translation history
- doctrinal and liturgical reception in major Christian traditions

## Why it belongs in the manuscript layer

The LXX should not be treated as identical with the Hebrew source text, but it also should not be ignored as a mere secondary translation witness.

It is a major textual and interpretive tradition whose differences from the Masoretic Text can matter for both exegesis and theological reception.

## Repository significance

The LXX is especially important for:
- comparing Old Testament witness traditions
- understanding patristic and Orthodox reception
- tracing where Greek renderings shape doctrinal language
- evaluating variant traditions where the Masoretic Text is not the only relevant witness

## Interpretive caution

The repository should treat the LXX as:
- an ancient and highly important witness
- a translation tradition with known theological significance
- a comparison partner to the Masoretic Text and other witnesses

It should not be flattened into either:
- a perfect replacement for the Hebrew text
- an irrelevant secondary source with no serious theological role
