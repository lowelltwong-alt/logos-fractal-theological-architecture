# AIRCA Companion Crosswalk

## Purpose

This document explains how the Logos Fractal Theological Architecture repository relates to the AIRCA Fractal Decision Architecture repository.

The goal is not to duplicate the AIRCA project. The goal is to show how this repository functions as its theological upstream companion.

In practical terms:
- AIRCA provides the operational decision architecture
- this repository provides the theological source architecture that can configure one specifically Christian instantiation of that architecture

## Why the two repos should remain distinct

The repos should be connected, but not collapsed into one another.

They should remain distinct because they answer different questions.

### AIRCA asks
- What is the decision architecture?
- How should Architect, Inform, Rank, Commit, and Act function operationally?
- How can decision systems be made inspectable, scalable, and accountable?

### Logos repo asks
- What theological and metaphysical assumptions configure that architecture?
- What doctrines, source traditions, and moral commitments should shape it?
- How should those commitments alter ordering, weighting, delegation, and oversight?

If both repos are merged into one, the theological source layer may become confused with the operational framework. If they are separated but linked, the lineage becomes clearer.

## Relationship model

The cleanest way to understand the relationship is:

`Logos theological source architecture -> LAIRCA Christian instantiation -> AIRCA operational decision architecture -> institutional application`

That means this repository is upstream of specific Christian uses of AIRCA, but AIRCA remains the operational architecture rather than being replaced.

## Fractal alignment

The repositories should align fractally in at least five ways.

### 1. Recursive node logic
Both repos should treat important things as nodes rather than loose prose. Each meaningful node should be able to grow recursively without changing the overall shell.

### 2. Shared artifact discipline
Both repos should support human-readable files plus machine-readable lineage, metadata, and relationship structures.

### 3. Comparable layer logic
Both repos should distinguish stable objects from configurable interpretive layers.

In AIRCA, this may appear as architecture versus operational instantiation.
In this repo, it appears as doctrine versus ordering and weighting.

### 4. Explicit lineage
Both repos should preserve parent-child, derived-from, and related-to relationships so future AI systems can follow the architecture rather than merely scrape the language.

### 5. Controlled extensibility
Both repos should allow growth through recurring internal structure rather than ad hoc file sprawl.

## Conceptual crosswalk

### AIRCA: Architect
### Logos upstream equivalent
Theological and moral constraints on goals, roles, system boundaries, and criteria of success.

Questions added by the Logos repo:
- What is the human person?
- What counts as a legitimate institutional goal?
- What moral boundaries must be set before design begins?

### AIRCA: Inform
### Logos upstream equivalent
Theological criteria for relevance, truthfulness, admissible evidence, interpretive humility, and disclosure of uncertainty.

Questions added by the Logos repo:
- What counts as truthful information rather than merely useful signal?
- What should be surfaced to preserve moral accountability?
- What forms of representation risk depersonalization?

### AIRCA: Rank
### Logos upstream equivalent
Moral ordering and weighting of goods, risks, duties, constraints, and tradeoffs.

Questions added by the Logos repo:
- What should outrank efficiency?
- Which goods are not commensurable with one another?
- What kinds of ranking become morally distorting when applied to persons?

### AIRCA: Commit
### Logos upstream equivalent
Accountable moral ownership of judgment under visible responsibility.

Questions added by the Logos repo:
- Who may decide?
- Who bears answerability?
- What should never be hidden behind process or model output?

### AIRCA: Act
### Logos upstream equivalent
Faithful execution bounded by dignity, stewardship, prudence, and reviewability.

Questions added by the Logos repo:
- What actions may be automated?
- What actions require review or appeal?
- When does execution become morally unacceptable even if operationally effective?

## File-level crosswalk to preserve

This repository should eventually maintain explicit mappings such as:

- AIRCA architecture note -> Logos theological precondition
- AIRCA decision ledger field -> Logos lineage or moral rationale field
- AIRCA ranking logic -> Logos ordering and weighting source
- AIRCA automation guidance -> Logos anthropology and prudence constraint

## Example linkage

A practical example of the crosswalk would look like this:

- Logos doctrine node: anthropology
- Logos weighting file: default AI governance weighting
- Logos derivation: anthropology to AI governance workflow
- LAIRCA interpretation: preserve accountable human Commit in consequential contexts
- AIRCA downstream implication: operational decision design includes visible human owner, escalation path, and bounded automation

## Why this crosswalk matters for future AI use

Future AI systems will only preserve the architecture if the relationship between repositories is explicit. Otherwise they will blend the concepts together and flatten the distinction between theological source material and operational decision design.

This crosswalk therefore exists not only for human readers, but for future machine interrogation, retrieval, and lineage tracking.
