# Genesis 11

## Why Genesis 11 matters

Genesis 11 matters because the Babel narrative is one of the repository's most important chapter-level nodes for thinking about centralized ambition, coordinated human power, false unity, language, and divine restraint.

For this project, it has major implications for theology, governance, institutions, public order, and AI-era questions about global coordination and scale.

## Why this chapter belongs early in the buildout

This chapter should be built early because it helps the repository reason about:
- concentrated human power
- unity pursued on false terms
- ambition for self-exaltation
- language and interpretive plurality
- God's disruption of self-authorizing systems
- the limits of scale without humility

## Key focal passage

The first high-priority text node in this chapter is:
- `text.genesis.11.1-9`

## Major interpretive questions

Genesis 11 raises several questions the repository will need to handle explicitly:
- whether Babel is mainly about pride, empire, false worship, centralized power, technics, or all of these together
- how language diversity should be read theologically
- whether divine scattering is punitive only, protective, or also providential
- how this text should shape Christian thinking about global systems, coordination, and authority

## Downstream significance

Genesis 11 strongly informs:
- biblical_theme.babel
- doctrines of pride and institutional limit
- public theology and political theology
- critiques of empire, false universality, and centralized domination
- AI governance questions around scale, coordination, and concentrated technical power
