# Genesis 1

## Why Genesis 1 matters

Genesis 1 is one of the most important chapter-level nodes in the repository because it frames creation as ordered and good, situates humanity within that order, and introduces image-bearing, authority, and vocation in a way that directly shapes the project's anthropology and governance logic.

## Why this chapter belongs early in the buildout

This chapter is essential because it connects directly to:
- image of God
- likeness and representation
- delegated authority
- stewardship and dominion
- creation as intelligible and ordered reality

These themes are foundational for any Christian account of AI, governance, and institutional design that wants to begin with theology rather than optimization alone.

## Key focal passage

The first high-priority text node in this chapter is:
- `text.genesis.1.26-28`

## Major interpretive questions

Genesis 1 raises several questions that the repository will need to handle explicitly:
- what it means for humans to be made in God's image and likeness
- what sort of authority is implied in dominion language
- how vocation and stewardship relate to creaturely dependence on God
- how this chapter should be read in relation to Genesis 2 and 3

## Downstream significance

Genesis 1 strongly informs:
- concept.imago_dei
- dignity and irreducibility of the person
- authority as delegated, not sovereign
- stewardship and vocation
- theological limits on reductive or exploitative models of human rule
