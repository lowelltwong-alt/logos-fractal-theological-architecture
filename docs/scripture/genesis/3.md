# Genesis 3

## Why Genesis 3 matters

Genesis 3 is one of the most important chapter-level nodes in the repository because it explains why disorder in persons, work, and institutions cannot be reduced to ignorance alone. It provides the scriptural ground for thinking about pride, misdirected desire, damaged willing, and the distortion of creaturely vocation.

## Why this chapter belongs early in the buildout

This chapter is essential because it directly informs:
- Augustine's account of disordered love
- Christian doctrines of sin and fallenness
- the difference between technical competence and moral wisdom
- labor as now marked by toil, frustration, and conflict
- the moral instability of human power and institutions

## Key focal passage

The first high-priority text node in this chapter is:
- `text.genesis.3.1-19`

## Major interpretive questions

Genesis 3 raises several questions the repository will need to handle explicitly:
- what kind of disorder enters through the fall
- whether the core problem is ignorance, rebellion, pride, desire, or all of these in relation
- how the curse affects labor, authority, and creaturely life
- how fallenness shapes human institutions and governance

## Downstream significance

Genesis 3 strongly informs:
- concept.sin
- concept.pride
- concept.work
- doctrines of fallenness and damaged willing
- anti-utopian limits on governance and AI optimism
- the claim that information increase does not automatically heal moral disorder
