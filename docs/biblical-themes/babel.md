# Babel

## Why this theme matters

Babel is one of the most important biblical themes in the repository for thinking about coordinated human ambition, centralized power, false universality, language, plurality, and divine restraint.

For this project, Babel matters because it speaks directly to questions of governance, institutional scale, empire, technological coordination, and the moral danger of unified systems ordered around self-exaltation rather than obedience to God.

## Core scriptural cluster

The primary early scriptural cluster for this theme is:
- Genesis 11:1–9

Other related passages and traditions may be added later as the theme expands.

## Major interpretive questions

This theme raises several major questions:
- whether Babel is best read as a story of pride, empire, false worship, technics, centralized coordination, or all of these together
- whether linguistic plurality should be read as judgment, protection, providential diversification, or a combination
- how divine restraint functions when human systems accumulate power too quickly or on false terms
- how this theme should inform Christian thinking about global governance and technological systems

## Downstream significance

This theme strongly informs:
- doctrines of institutional limit
- critiques of false unity and self-authorizing scale
- public theology and political theology
- reflections on language, translation, and plural publics
- AI governance questions concerning centralization, coordination, and control

## Repository use

In this repository, the Babel theme should function as a bridge between:
- biblical text
- doctrines of pride and judgment
- institutional theology
- public theology
- governance and AI applications involving scale and coordinated power
