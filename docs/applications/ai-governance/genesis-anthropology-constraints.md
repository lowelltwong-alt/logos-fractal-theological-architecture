# Genesis Anthropology Constraints for AI Governance

## Purpose

This node translates the Genesis anthropology reference slice into downstream constraints for AI governance.

## Core claim

If the human person is treated as image-bearing, dignity-bearing, and vocation-bearing in a thick theological sense, then AI governance should not reduce persons to mere output profiles, score vectors, risk buckets, or optimization targets.

## First constraints

### 1. Non-reduction constraint
Systems should not treat the human person as fully exhaustible by machine-legible attributes.

### 2. Delegation constraint
Automation may assist judgment, but should not erase accountable human responsibility in dignity-sensitive decisions.

### 3. Stewardship constraint
Power over tools, systems, and classifications should be exercised as delegated stewardship rather than as sovereign technical domination.

### 4. Vocation constraint
Work, responsibility, and role are not merely cost variables. Governance should preserve the human meaning of work and responsibility.

### 5. Review constraint
High-impact uses of AI involving persons should preserve review, contestability, and accountable oversight.
