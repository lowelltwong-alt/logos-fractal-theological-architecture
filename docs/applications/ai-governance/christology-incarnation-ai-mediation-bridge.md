## Theological source claim set

### Direct theological claims carried into this bridge

- Christian Christology and the incarnation matter for how mediation, embodiment, and accountable presence are understood.
- AI-generated communication is not the same thing as embodied personal, pastoral, sacramental, or ecclesial presence.
- Human accountability should remain visible where speech, counsel, or representation materially affects persons.

### Prudential governance applications derived from those claims

- Material AI mediation should be disclosed in contexts where users could mistake generated output for embodied human presence.
- AI should not impersonate a pastor, elder, priest, counselor, church office, sacramental minister, or embodied community.
- Consequential pastoral, spiritual, disciplinary, or care decisions should preserve named human ownership.

## Governance implications

- Institutional AI use should preserve disclosure of mediated authorship where the distinction matters.
- Systems should distinguish assistance, representation, and authoritative human presence rather than flattening them into one channel.
- Communication workflows should preserve accountable human review before AI-generated material is used in sensitive pastoral or ecclesial contexts.
- Later tradition-specific work may need stronger boundaries for sacramental, liturgical, or ecclesial office contexts.

## What the bridge permits

- AI assistance for drafting, summarization, preparation, and internal workflow support.
- Use of AI as an aid to communication when human ownership and disclosure remain visible.
- Later tradition-scoped refinement about pastoral or ecclesial use after this draft is reviewed.

## What the bridge forbids

- False presentation of AI output as embodied human presence.
- Impersonation of pastoral, ecclesial, or sacramental office without clear disclosure and authorized human ownership.
- Treating this bridge as if it were itself Christology or final ecclesial doctrine.
- Auto-promoting research-seed claims into graph or claim-object form in this pass.

## Tradition-scope caution

This bridge does not attempt to settle all Christian differences around office, mediation, sacrament, or pastoral representation.

Those differences matter, and later work should make them explicit instead of pretending one application rule fits every tradition identically.
