## Theological source claim set

### Direct theological claims carried into this bridge

- Christian doctrine of the Trinity matters upstream of anthropology and cannot be treated as irrelevant to downstream accounts of personhood.
- Human beings are not reducible to machine-legible function, productivity, or optimization value.
- Human moral agency and dignity require accountable treatment in consequential institutional decisions.

### Prudential governance applications derived from those claims

- AI-assisted systems should not present operational descriptions as if they exhaust human identity or worth.
- Consequential uses of AI should preserve named human decision ownership.
- Institutions should preserve explanation, review, and challenge paths where AI materially affects persons.

## Governance implications

- High-consequence AI workflows should preserve accountable human ownership at the final commit point.
- Ranking, classification, and recommendation systems should avoid totalizing person-level profiles.
- Institutional AI use should preserve room for contextual judgment rather than pretending the model has captured the whole person.
- Review posture should remain cautious where one tradition may derive stronger or weaker implications from Trinitarian personhood language.

## What the bridge permits

- Using AI to assist review, triage, summarization, and recommendation.
- Building downstream governance constraints that remain visibly traceable to upstream doctrine and anthropology.
- Tradition-scoped follow-on work after steward review.
- Later claim objects or graph objects only after the prose derivation is accepted and source review is stronger.

## What the bridge forbids

- Treating machine-readable profiles as exhaustive judgments about persons.
- Letting automation erase named human responsibility in consequential decisions.
- Presenting this bridge as if it were itself doctrine, creed, or final authority.
- Auto-promoting incoming research language into claim objects or graph relations without a separate review step.

## Tradition-scope caution

This bridge is written as a shared-core draft application slice, not as a claim that all Christian traditions express personhood or communion in identical conceptual language.

Any stronger tradition-specific move should be marked with explicit tradition scope in a later pass.
