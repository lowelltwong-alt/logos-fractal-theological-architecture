# Math Frontier Watch Policy

## Purpose

Monitor frontier math and formal verification because they may eventually improve:

- graph theory;
- category-theoretic or type-theoretic architecture;
- proof assistants;
- claim validation;
- schema validation;
- graph invariants;
- retrieval/ranking;
- compression;
- formal models of trust zones and source hierarchy.

## High-value domains

- logic and proof theory;
- type theory;
- category theory;
- graph theory and network science;
- order/lattice theory;
- topology and sheaf theory;
- probability and Bayesian inference;
- information theory;
- causal inference;
- optimization;
- complexity theory;
- formal language theory;
- linear algebra and embeddings;
- statistics and experimental design;
- control theory and dynamical systems;
- game theory and mechanism design.

## Required handling

A math signal must be staged as research unless it is only a watch note.

Do not implement new math architecture, validators, proof assistants, or formalized doctrine logic without a separate route and review.

## Review escalation

Require review from an ontology/validation owner before applying math results to schemas, validators, graph objects, claim structures, source registries, or routing.
