# First LAIRCA Doctrinal Cluster: Justification

This note establishes the first doctrinal-cluster entry point under LAIRCA.

## Why this belongs here

AIRCA should remain primarily secular-business and governance oriented.

When doctrinal material is being used to configure a specifically Christian decision architecture, it belongs under **LAIRCA** inside the broader Logos project rather than in the AIRCA repository.

## Why justification is a good pilot

Justification is a strong pilot doctrinal cluster because it is:

- central to Christian theology
- historically contested
- highly connected to anthropology, grace, sanctification, sacrament, and assurance
- suitable for demonstrating how doctrine can affect decision architecture indirectly through weighting, constraints, and governance assumptions

## What this cluster should eventually contain

- doctrine topic definition
- competing doctrinal views
- tradition-scoped assessments
- provenance-bearing assertions
- coherence and contradiction mapping
- doctrine-to-governance implications
- LAIRCA crosswalk to Architect, Inform, Rank, Commit, and Act

## Boundary discipline

The purpose here is not to turn LAIRCA into the entirety of Logos.

LAIRCA should receive doctrinal material insofar as that material is being used to shape Christian decision architecture. Broader doctrine, canon, scripture, and theological-source work still belongs to the larger Logos architecture beyond LAIRCA alone.

## Immediate next step

Build the justification cluster as a LAIRCA-configuring doctrinal object, not merely as a free-floating theology note.
