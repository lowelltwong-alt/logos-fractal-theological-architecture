# Phased Build Architecture

## Phase 1 — Constitutional Core

### What belongs here
- Governance contract and constitutional doctrine rules
- Trust-zone boundaries and lifecycle semantics
- Core object separation rules (topic, view, assessment)

### Why it matters
Without constitutional constraints, later doctrinal growth becomes inconsistent and hard to audit.

### Output
A stable governance base that defines what can be added, how trust is scoped, and how identity is preserved.

## Phase 2 — Source and Interpretation Layer

### What belongs here
- Source-bearing objects
- Passage interpretation objects
- Clear links from doctrine work back to source and interpretation material

### Why it matters
It prevents doctrinal conclusions from being confused with raw source data.

### Output
A traceable path from source to interpretation to doctrine-facing objects.

## Phase 3 — Pilot Doctrine Pattern

### What belongs here
- A single doctrine-topic pilot with multiple views
- Baseline-scoped assessments
- Explicit relationship objects for topic-view, assessment, grounding, and view-view links

### Why it matters
It demonstrates the architecture's visible advantage: disagreement is represented without identity collapse.

### Output
A legible pilot doctrine network (Trinity) that keeps topic, view, and judgment distinct.

## Phase 4 — Relationship Density and Network Legibility

### What belongs here
- More relationship objects per doctrine area
- Relationship manifests and compact network summaries
- Cross-topic bridges where doctrinal dependencies are explicit

### Why it matters
Dense but clear relationship mapping makes the architecture navigable for humans and machines.

### Output
Multi-topic relationship graphs with readable manifests and stronger interconnection clarity.

## Phase 5 — Coherence Engine and Tension Mapping

### What belongs here
- Rule-based coherence checks across views and assessments
- Tension markers and contradiction surfaces
- Baseline-relative coherence reporting

### Why it matters
The project needs structured ways to name tensions without flattening doctrinal diversity.

### Output
A reproducible coherence/tension layer that can be inspected and refined.

## Phase 6 — Scholar and Editor Workflow Layer

### What belongs here
- Structured contributor workflows for proposals, review, and promotion
- Editorial checkpoints for trust-zone transitions
- History-preserving deprecation and replacement procedures

### Why it matters
Scalability requires explicit workflows, not ad hoc edits.

### Output
A maintainable governance workflow where additions and promotions remain transparent.

## Phase 7 — AI Retrieval and Proposal Quarantine

### What belongs here
- Retrieval surfaces that expose only appropriate trust zones
- Quarantine lanes for AI-generated proposals
- Review gates before any promotion to higher-trust zones

### Why it matters
AI assistance can accelerate drafting, but governance must prevent unreviewed material from becoming canonical.

### Output
Safe AI-assisted drafting and retrieval patterns aligned with trust-zone policy.

## Phase 8 — Publication, Expansion, and Comparative Scale

### What belongs here
- Publication-ready exports and documentation views
- Broader tradition coverage and comparative mappings
- Cross-project integration surfaces for reuse

### Why it matters
The architecture should serve both internal governance and external comparative theological work.

### Output
A publishable, extensible, comparative theological knowledge architecture.

## How the phases work together

The phases form a dependency chain:

- Phase 1 governs identity and trust
- Phase 2 secures source-to-doctrine traceability
- Phase 3 proves the doctrine pattern on one pilot network
- Phase 4 scales relationship clarity across topics
- Phase 5 evaluates coherence and tension explicitly
- Phase 6 operationalizes human workflow
- Phase 7 integrates AI under quarantine and review
- Phase 8 packages and scales the system for broad use

Each phase constrains and enables the next, so growth remains structurally disciplined.
