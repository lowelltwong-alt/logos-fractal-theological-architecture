# Imago Dei and Human Dignity

Author: Lowell T. Wong  
Status: draft  
Node type: doctrine  
Canonical ID: doctrine.imago_dei.human_dignity

## Summary

Human beings bear the image of God and therefore possess inherent dignity that cannot be reduced to efficiency, utility, optimization value, or machine-like interchangeability.

## Why this doctrine matters for Christian AI discernment

This doctrine is one of the most important starting points for evaluating AI because it shapes how Christians think about:
- replacement versus augmentation
- dignity in work
- manipulation and deception
- depersonalization in service delivery
- whether humans may be treated primarily as data subjects, behavior targets, or workflow units

## Core claim

A Christian approach to AI should not evaluate people primarily as throughput variables. Human beings remain morally significant persons, not just inputs into a more efficient system.

## Plain-language meaning

In simple terms:
- people matter because they are made by God
- a person is worth more than what they produce
- efficiency is not the highest good
- systems that reduce people to utility should be treated with suspicion

## AI implications

### Stable implications across most Christian paths
- AI should not be justified purely by labor elimination
- systems should protect meaningful human responsibility
- deception about whether something is human or not can matter morally
- vulnerable people should not be treated as optimization targets

### Questions this doctrine raises
- does this AI system increase or diminish human dignity?
- does it preserve meaningful human agency?
- does it make humans easier to manage at the cost of personhood?
- does it invite relational confusion or depersonalization?
