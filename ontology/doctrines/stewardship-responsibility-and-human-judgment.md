# Stewardship, Responsibility, and Human Judgment

Author: Lowell T. Wong  
Status: draft  
Node type: doctrine  
Canonical ID: doctrine.stewardship.responsibility.human_judgment

## Summary

Christians are called to steward tools, power, and institutions responsibly. AI may support judgment, but it does not remove human accountability before God.

## Why this doctrine matters for Christian AI discernment

Many AI systems promise speed, automation, and delegation. This doctrine helps determine what may be delegated, what must remain under accountable human judgment, and what kinds of convenience become moral abdication.

## Core claim

AI may inform human action, but it should not be allowed to dissolve responsibility. Human beings remain answerable for how systems are designed, ranked, deployed, and used.

## Plain-language meaning

In simple terms:
- tools can help, but they do not take your moral place
- convenience is not the same as faithfulness
- delegation still leaves someone responsible
- people cannot hide behind the phrase the system decided

## AI implications

### Stable implications across most Christian paths
- high-impact decisions need accountable human oversight
- automation should not erase ownership of outcomes
- ranking systems and decision systems need clear governance
- Christians should be cautious when AI becomes a shield against responsibility

### Questions this doctrine raises
- who remains accountable for this output or decision?
- what judgment is being delegated?
- what oversight exists?
- is this a support tool or a responsibility-evasion tool?
