# Eastern Orthodox Starting Profile

Author: Lowell T. Wong  
Status: draft  
Node type: tradition_stance  
Canonical ID: tradition.eastern_orthodox.starting_profile

## Summary

A starter tradition profile for users approaching Christian AI discernment from an Eastern Orthodox framework that emphasizes personhood, spiritual formation, ascetic discipline, communion, and resistance to dehumanizing abstraction.

## Why this profile matters

Orthodox reasoning may weigh AI issues differently because it often gives strong attention to what technologies do to the soul, the habits, the relationships, and the person rather than only whether an output is efficient or technically permissible.

## Common characteristics
- strong emphasis on personhood over abstraction
- strong concern for spiritual formation and the passions
- caution toward technological mediation that weakens embodied or ecclesial life
- concern for humility, discernment, and non-domination

## Likely AI emphasis areas
- spiritual formation and dependence
- false intimacy and pseudo-communion
- disordered desire and distraction
- personhood versus reductionism
- humility, ascetic restraint, and discernment

## Risks in discernment
- reducing the tradition to vague mysticism
- missing the importance of formation and embodied practice
- over-translating everything into modern secular categories

## Best system use
Use this profile when the user:
- identifies as Orthodox
- wants strong attention to formation and personhood
- wants guidance on how technology may deform attention, humility, or communion
