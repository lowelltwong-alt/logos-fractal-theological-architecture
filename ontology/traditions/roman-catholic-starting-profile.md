## Summary

A starter tradition profile for users approaching Christian AI discernment from a Roman Catholic framework that gives meaningful weight to Scripture, tradition, the Church, moral theology, and the formation of persons and communities.

## Why this profile matters

Catholic users often have stronger categories for natural law, the common good, institutional authority, human dignity, and formation than minimally specified non-denominational users. This can materially affect AI judgments.

## Common characteristics
- strong emphasis on human dignity and the moral status of the person
- concern for natural law and the integrity of human nature
- stronger role for church tradition and moral reasoning
- concern for technology’s effects on virtue, social order, and the common good
- willingness to think institutionally, not only individually

## Likely AI emphasis areas
- anthropological integrity
- truth and moral formation
- relational and sexual disorder
- subsidiarity, solidarity, and the common good
- institutional responsibility and governance

## Risks in discernment
- over-formalizing issues a lay user needs in simpler language
- assuming the user knows the tradition’s reasoning vocabulary already

## Best system use
Use this profile when the user:
- identifies as Catholic
- wants tradition-aware guidance
- cares about Church teaching, natural law, and moral theology
