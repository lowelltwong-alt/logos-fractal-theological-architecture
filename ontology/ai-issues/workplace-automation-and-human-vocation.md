# Workplace Automation and Human Vocation

Author: Lowell T. Wong  
Status: draft  
Node type: ai_issue  
Canonical ID: ai_issue.workplace_automation.human_vocation

## Summary

This issue concerns the use of AI to automate work, compress roles, eliminate tasks, and redesign organizations in ways that affect dignity, vocation, meaningful participation, and responsibility.

## Why this is a priority issue

Many Christians will first encounter AI as a work question rather than a church question. This makes workplace automation one of the most practically important discernment areas.

## Core discernment question

When does automation function as good stewardship that reduces unnecessary burden, and when does it become a dignity-reducing system that weakens meaningful human participation?

## Stable concerns across many Christian paths
- efficiency alone is not a sufficient moral test
- humans should not be treated merely as cost centers
- meaningful work and responsible participation matter
- institutions should not hide behind optimization language to avoid moral accountability

## Doctrines most relevant
- doctrine.imago_dei.human_dignity
- doctrine.stewardship.responsibility.human_judgment
- vocation and meaningful work

## Tradition-sensitive areas
- some traditions will emphasize the common good and institutional obligations more strongly
- some will emphasize vocation, neighbor-love, and stewardship in employer obligations
- broadly evangelical users may respond strongly when automation appears to erode dignity or honesty

## Guidance direction

Likely lower-risk cases:
- removing repetitive low-value burden while preserving human judgment
- augmenting workers into more meaningful and capable roles
- broadening access to useful tools and training

Likely higher-risk cases:
- using AI mainly to reduce labor while concealing that motive
- concentrating decision power while weakening worker agency
- treating employees as validators of machine output with little judgment left
- automating sensitive decisions with weak accountability

## Questions to ask
- is this tool elevating human work or hollowing it out?
- who benefits from the productivity gain?
- what responsibilities remain human and visible?
- are workers being developed or merely squeezed?
