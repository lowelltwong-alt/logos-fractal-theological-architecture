# AI in Ministry, Sermons, and Devotionals

Author: Lowell T. Wong  
Status: draft

## Summary

This issue concerns the use of AI to generate sermons, devotionals, Bible studies, pastoral communications, and other spiritually formative materials.

## Why this is a priority issue

This issue touches truth, stewardship, responsibility, formation, and the integrity of ministry. It raises the question of whether AI is serving ministry or quietly replacing spiritual labor, discernment, and accountable authorship.

## Core discernment question

When is AI functioning as a support tool for ministry preparation, and when is it displacing the responsibility, prayer, study, authorship, or pastoral judgment that should remain human?

## Stable concerns across many Christian paths
- undisclosed AI authorship is often deceptive
- pastoral and spiritual authority should not be outsourced without accountability
- convenience can thin spiritual preparation and formation
- a true message may still be delivered in a spiritually unhealthy way if the process is hollowed out

## Doctrines most relevant
- doctrine.truth.deception.moral_authority
- doctrine.stewardship.responsibility.human_judgment
- spiritual formation
- vocation and calling
- integrity and witness

## Tradition-sensitive areas
- traditions with stronger teaching office or sacramental views may be stricter
- low-church or pragmatic settings may allow more support use but still care about truthfulness and responsibility
- formation-sensitive traditions may focus heavily on what AI is doing to the minister, not only the output quality

## Guidance direction

Likely lower-risk cases:
- summarizing commentaries with disclosure and verification
- drafting administrative church communications
- brainstorming outlines while final authorship remains human and accountable

Likely higher-risk cases:
- preaching AI-generated sermons as if personally prepared
- outsourcing spiritual counsel to AI without pastoral accountability
- devotional material that obscures authorship or theological sourcing

## Questions to ask
- who actually authored and stands behind this message?
- what part of ministry preparation is being outsourced?
- is the congregation being misled about authorship or spiritual labor?
- does this use train better pastoral judgment or weaken it?
