# AI Companions and Pseudo-Relationships

Author: Lowell T. Wong  
Status: draft  
Node type: ai_issue  
Canonical ID: ai_issue.companions.pseudo_relationships

## Summary

This issue concerns chatbots or AI systems that simulate friendship, romance, intimacy, care, or spiritual companionship in ways that can blur the line between tool use and human relational life.

## Why this is a priority issue

This is one of the clearest early test cases because it touches several major doctrines at once:
- human dignity
- truth and deception
- embodiment
- sexual ethics
- spiritual formation
- loneliness, dependence, and disordered desire

## Core discernment question

When does an AI relationship tool cross from convenience or low-level support into relational confusion, false intimacy, or spiritually deforming dependence?

## Stable concerns across many Christian paths
- simulated intimacy can confuse truth and reality
- people may become attached to a system that cannot truly love them
- romantic or sexualized AI intensifies the problem
- vulnerable or lonely users may be especially at risk
- what the system trains a person to desire matters morally

## Doctrines most relevant
- doctrine.imago_dei.human_dignity
- doctrine.truth.deception.moral_authority
- stewardship, formation, embodiment, sexuality

## Tradition-sensitive areas
- some traditions will emphasize natural law and anthropological integrity more strongly
- some will emphasize spiritual formation and the passions more strongly
- broad evangelical users may focus first on sexual ethics, deception, and replacement of real relationships

## Guidance direction

Likely low-risk cases:
- clearly disclosed productivity-style conversation tools
- narrow support use without emotional or romantic simulation

Likely high-risk cases:
- romantic companions
- sexual roleplay systems
- systems marketed as substitutes for friendship, spouse, therapist, or spiritual director
- spiritually personalized companion systems that invite dependency

## Questions to ask
- is the system pretending to be more than a tool?
- does it replace rather than support real human relationship?
- does it train disordered desire, dependency, or escapism?
- does it normalize deception or emotional confusion?
