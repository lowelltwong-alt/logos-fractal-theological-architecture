# Research Packet: Human Person and Formation Core Slice

## 1. Architectural thesis

The Human Person and Formation Core Slice should make explicit that a Christian decision architecture cannot treat humans as throughput units, behavioral surfaces, or passive validators of machine output. Human beings are image-bearing, morally accountable, formable, vulnerable, finite, fallen, and capable of vocation, lament, hope, and grace-enabled transformation.

This slice should therefore become the bridge between:

```text
doctrine.anthropology
-> concept.imago_dei / concept.sin / concept.vocation / concept.grace / concept.formation
-> AI governance constraints
-> later claim objects and graph relationship objects
```

## 3. Concept architecture

### 3.1 `personhood`

Definition direction: the theological category naming the human person as more than organism, role, dataset, or choice engine. Personhood includes creatureliness, relationality, rationality, will, memory/interiority, moral accountability, and dignity before God.

Primary links:

- informed by `doctrine.anthropology`
- grounded by `concept.imago_dei`
- sharpened by Augustine's interiority and will
- constrained by Christology/incarnation later

AI governance use:

- humans must not be reduced to scores or workflow units;
- automation must preserve responsible human agency;
- systems should be evaluated by what they form in people, not only by output.

### 3.2 `human_agency`

Definition direction: the capacity and responsibility of human persons to attend, judge, choose, act, repent, receive grace, and remain accountable.

Important distinction:

- agency is not autonomous sovereignty;
- agency is creaturely, accountable, damaged by fallenness, and restorable under grace.

AI governance use:

- decision ownership cannot be delegated to model output;
- important actions need accountable human Commit/Act points;
- automation can support action but should not obscure who is responsible.

### 3.3 `imago_dei`

Existing node should remain central.

Deepening direction:

- keep Genesis 1:26-28 and lexical/source-basis work;
- connect image to dignity, responsibility, representation/stewardship, and not just individual capacity;
- add Athanasius as an image-renewal anchor.

### 3.4 `fallenness` / `sin`

The repo already has `concept.sin`. Treat fallenness as an alias or subtheme unless Codex confirms the repo distinguishes them.

Deepening direction:

- Genesis 3 is not merely ignorance; it is disorder of love, pride, blame-shifting, mistrust, and damaged willing;
- AI governance must assume systems can scale self-justification, institutional drift, and rationalized domination.

### 3.5 `ordered_love`

Definition direction: Augustine's pattern that holiness and justice require loving the right things in the right order, with God as the proper highest good and neighbor-love ordered toward God.

AI governance use:

- efficiency, scale, relevance, and control are goods only when subordinated to higher goods;
- misordered systems can optimize the wrong thing very effectively.

### 3.6 `suffering_and_lament`

Definition direction: Christian anthropology must include suffering, grief, and lament as the truthful speech of finite and wounded persons before God, not merely as data noise or user dissatisfaction.

Source direction:

- Lamentations 3 as a key lament/hope anchor;
- Romans 8 as groaning/hope/creation-level suffering anchor;
- Nouwen as a modern servant-leadership and wounded-vulnerability bridge.

AI governance use:

- do not flatten suffering into sentiment or complaint classification;
- do not route vulnerable states into automated pastoral/clinical workflows;
- include lament as an evidence category for human reality without turning it into diagnosis.

### 3.7 `hope`

Definition direction: hope is not optimism or prediction confidence. It is trust in God's faithful restoration amid suffering, finitude, and incompletion.

AI governance use:

- reject both despair and techno-utopianism;
- preserve limits while allowing constructive action;
- frame AI as subordinate tool, never savior language.

### 3.8 `formation`

Definition direction: persons are formed by repeated practices, attention, habits, loves, and institutional patterns. Systems train people; they do not merely help people execute tasks.

Source direction:

- Willard as primary modern source anchor;
- Augustine as ordered-love/deep anthropology anchor;
- Nouwen as leadership-formation/power anchor.

AI governance use:

- ask what kind of people and institutions a system forms;
- preserve attentiveness, responsibility, humility, and disciplined judgment;
- evaluate deskilling and dependency risks.

### 3.9 `vocation / work`

Existing `concept.vocation` should be audited rather than duplicated.

Deepening direction:

- Keller: work as meaningful service, not only self-interest or productivity;
- Luther later: calling and ordinary duties as neighbor-serving work under faith;
- Calvin later: governance seriousness and ordered duties.

AI governance use:

- automation should relieve toil without emptying meaningful work;
- work should remain participatory and neighbor-directed;
- labor reduction alone is not a sufficient theological success metric.

### 3.10 `grace`

Definition direction: grace is not a vague positive force. It is God's restoring, forgiving, renewing action, necessary because fallenness cannot be solved by technique, information, or institutional design alone.

Source direction:

- Augustine: grace is necessary because human willing is damaged;
- Athanasius: incarnation and restoration/recreation of image;
- Calvin/Luther later: grace/justification/fallenness with care and tradition-scoped framing.

AI governance use:

- systems cannot redeem institutions;
- governance should be humble, reviewable, and non-utopian;
- technical fixes should not be treated as moral healing.

## 4. Public source spine

Use these as initial public research anchors. Do not overquote copyrighted modern works. Prefer short summary and source pointers.

### Scripture anchors

- Genesis 1:26-28 — image, dominion/stewardship, human responsibility.
- Genesis 3:1-19 — fallenness, mistrust, shame, blame, toil.
- Psalm/lament corpus — human grief and truthful prayer.
- Lamentations 3:21-24 — hope inside lament.
- Romans 8:18-30 — suffering, groaning, hope, redemption of creation/body.
- Ephesians 1:7; 2:8-10 — grace, redemption, workmanship, good works.

### Augustine

- *Confessions*, Book I.1 — restlessness of the heart and creaturely orientation to God.
- *On Christian Doctrine*, Book I, especially chapters on use/enjoyment and order of love.
- *City of God*, especially two-cities / governing loves logic.

### Athanasius

- *On the Incarnation*, especially image renewal, corruption/death, and the Word restoring human nature.

### Dallas Willard

- *Renovation of the Heart* — the human self, heart/will, and formation as reordering of the person under God.

### Henri Nouwen

- *In the Name of Jesus* — leadership temptations, vulnerability, prayer, ministry, and non-dominating power.

### Tim Keller

- *Every Good Endeavor* — work as service, meaning, vocation, and worship rather than self-interest alone.

### Calvin / Luther later

- Calvin: *Institutes*, Book I.1 and I.15; later Book II.1 for fallenness; use carefully.
- Luther: *Treatise on Good Works* and vocation/calling material; use later to deepen work/vocation without overloading this slice.
