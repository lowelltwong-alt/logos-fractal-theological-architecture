# Doctrine Nodes

## Purpose

This folder holds doctrine nodes for the concordance graph.

Doctrine nodes are stable theological objects that may draw on many verses, passages, and concepts and may also become sites of interpretive disagreement or tradition-specific emphasis.

## Core rule

Do not confuse doctrine nodes with verses, topics, or broad concept labels.

A doctrine node should be used when the object is clearly theological and stable enough to justify its own node in the graph.

## What belongs here

- stable doctrines such as creation, resurrection, covenant theology, justification
- scripture support patterns with provenance and review state
- links to concepts, passages, and interpretive nodes

## Summary principle

Doctrine nodes give the concordance a disciplined theological layer, but they should be added more carefully than low-risk verse and entity links.
