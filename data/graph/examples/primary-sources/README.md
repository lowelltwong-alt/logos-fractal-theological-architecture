# Primary Sources Example Corpus

## Purpose

This folder holds small example object sets showing what a future primary-sources buildout could look like in practice.

These examples are not a complete manuscript corpus. They are demonstration slices meant to show how the repository can move from:
- witness or fragment
- to transcription
- to passage reconstruction
- to lexical evidence
- to translation comparison
- to governed relationship objects

without collapsing those layers into one another.

## Current sample sets

- `genesis-11/` — Old Testament sample showing a fuller Hebrew witness and a fragmentary Genesis witness example
- `mark-1/` — New Testament sample showing a Greek codex witness with page/viewer linkage

## Why these examples matter

These example sets help a future lead or team see:
- how object templates may be instantiated
- how provenance and confidence fields should behave
- how image and viewer references can fit the ontology
- how lexical and translation layers connect to textual witnesses

## Summary principle

A small governed example corpus is one of the best ways to make a future large corpus buildout understandable, testable, and handoff-ready.
