# Relationships Scheme

## Purpose

This file describes the relationships domain within the graph and concordance layer.

A relationships scheme groups governed edge and relationship objects that make important links explicit, reviewable, and reusable.

## Why this scheme exists

Not every link should become a first-class object.
But important links should not remain hidden, untyped, or structurally inconsistent.

A scheme gives governed relationship objects:
- bounded domain identity
- controlled relation semantics
- clearer provenance expectations
- easier validation and reuse

## What belongs here

Typical objects in this scheme include governed edges such as:
- verse-to-verse intertextual claims
- verse-to-concept grounding claims
- doctrine-support edges
- translation-sensitive relationship objects
- supersedable or contested high-value edges

## Relation to the rest of the repository

Relationship objects in this scheme should connect strongly to:
- graph node objects
- scripture spans
- concept objects
- doctrine objects
- provenance and review logic

## Summary principle

A relationships scheme helps the repository treat important connections as governed graph objects rather than hidden ad hoc links.
