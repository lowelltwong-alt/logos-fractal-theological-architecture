# Primary Sources Scheme

## Purpose

This file describes the future primary-sources domain within the graph and concordance layer.

A primary-sources scheme groups machine-readable witness, fragment, transcription, reconstruction, lexical-evidence, and translation-comparison objects so they can be governed as a bounded domain rather than as scattered imports.

## Why this scheme exists

A future biblical primary-sources corpus will be too large and too sensitive to remain an unstructured collection.

A scheme gives it:
- bounded domain identity
- clearer governance
- easier validation
- better provenance control
- safer linkage into scripture, doctrine, and theology

## What belongs here

Typical future objects in this scheme include:
- witness objects
- fragment objects
- transcription objects
- passage reconstruction objects
- lexical evidence objects
- translation comparison objects

## Relation to the rest of the repository

Objects in this scheme should connect strongly to:
- scripture nodes
- lexical nodes
- translation witnesses
- manuscript witnesses
- concept and doctrine objects
- relationship objects
- trust and provenance logic

## Summary principle

A primary-sources scheme helps the repository grow toward a manuscript-and-translation corpus without losing the discipline of the wider ontology.
